Nouns

A noun represents, place, animal, thing or an idea.

Kinds of noun

Nouns are classifide as follows: Proper: It is the name of particular object, whether person, place, animal, idea or thing. Common: It is the name possessed by things in general. Gollective: It is the name representing a group of things. Abstract: It is the name representing qualities, emotion, conditions or actions. Pronouns A pronuon is a word used in place of a noun. A pronoun is generally used to avoid repetition of a noun. Kinds of pronoun Pronouns are classified as follows: Personal pronouns: It refers to a specific noun and used as substitute of the noun. Interrogative pronouns: It stands as the answer to the question about a person, place or a thing. Inderfinite pronouns: It is used to refer to a person, thing or a place that are unknow. Relative pronouns: It relates to a noun, pronoun, word or expression preceding it in the sentence, and at the same time connects two statements. Demonstrative pronouns: A noun or pronoun is specifide and identified by it. Reflexive pronouns: It usally follows a noun or a pronoun and used to emphasize the subject.
